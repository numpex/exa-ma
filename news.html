Trans-Numériques 2026

Feb 02-05, 2026 | Couvent des Jacobins, Rennes

First edition bringing together France’s academic community around digital continuum challenges. NumPEx will be represented with presentations on performance, sustainability, and HPC. Exa-MA participants include project coordinators and WP leaders.

MAJSC 2026: ML & Autodiff in JAX

Jun 08-12, 2026 | IRMA, Université de Strasbourg

Workshop on Machine Learning and Automatic Differentiation in JAX for Scientific Computing. Five-day intensive program covering JAX fundamentals, neural networks (Equinox/Flax), automatic differentiation for PDEs (Diffrax), physics-informed neural networks, and neural operators. Organized by E. Franck (WP2 leader), V. Michel-Dansac, V. Vigon, and A. Musik from IRMA Strasbourg.

Three-day annual meeting with all WP leaders (WP1-WP7) presenting progress and scientific highlights. Featured breakout sessions with Feel++, Scimba, Composyx, and Uranie frameworks, plus presentations on Sage-HPC and Daimos. All 24 presentation slides are now available.

Jan 14, 2026

Exa-MA Website Refresh
The Exa-MA project website has been refreshed with updated content and improved navigation. New features include detailed work package pages, a comprehensive software stack catalog with 16+ open-source packages, team directory with photos, 2025 scientific highlights (50× GenEO speedups, 8000 GPU scale on LUMI, neural operator acceleration), and streamlined access to deliverables and publications.

Dec 09, 2025

Scientific Advisory Board Meeting – Virtual (Zoom)
Presentation of Exa-MA advances and 2025 highlights to the NumPEx Scientific Advisory Board. Topics included the 50× GenEO speedup, 8000-GPU LUMI deployment, neural operator acceleration (80-200% CPU savings), and CGAL 6.0 urban meshing achievements. Joint session with other NumPEx projects showcasing program-wide progress. Interactive session with board members providing feedback on project direction and scientific impact.

Nov 12-14, 2025

First YoungPEx General Meeting – ENSEEIHT, Toulouse
Three-day workshop bringing together young researchers to discuss the future of HPC and open science. Topics included sustainable computing with Georges Da Costa (Exa-SoFT), Grid'5000 tutorials, energy measurement, and workshops on social and gender equality in science. Organized by Thomas Saigre (Exa-MA), Karmijn Hoogveld (Exa-SofT), and other NumPEx members.
Event details and presentations

Nov 04, 2025

HiDALGO2 Clustering Event – HLRS, Stuttgart
Christophe Prud’homme presented the Ktirio Urban Building pilot for city energy modeling. The pilot, co-funded 50% by NumPEx for EuroHPC, showcased the Ktirio Geom library developed jointly through CoE Hidalgo2 and Exa-MA (WP1 Geometry, Unistra-Inria CA collaboration).
Event details and presentations

Sep 03, 2025

NumPEx/ANR Meeting – Siège de l’ANR, Paris
Presentation of Exa-MA advances and 2025 highlights to ANR (Agence Nationale de la Recherche) alongside other NumPEx projects. Joint session with all NumPEx project coordinators demonstrating the program’s scientific impact and progress towards exascale computing goals.
