= Exa-MA 2026 General Assembly
:page-tags: resource,news2026
:parent-catalogs: exama-home
:page-illustration: fa-solid fa-users
:description: Annual consortium meeting with all WP leaders at Arts et Métiers ParisTech, Aix-en-Provence.

[.lead]
The Exa-MA 2026 General Assembly took place from January 19-21, 2026, at Arts et Métiers ParisTech in Aix-en-Provence. This three-day event brought together the entire consortium for comprehensive progress reviews, framework presentations, and strategic planning.

== Overview

The General Assembly featured:

* **WP Progress Reviews**: All six work packages (WP1-WP6) presented scientific highlights and next steps
* **Framework Presentations**: Deep dives into Feel++, Scimba, Composyx, and Uranie frameworks
* **Breakout Sessions**: Focused collaboration sessions connecting frameworks with work packages
* **AI & HPC Integration**: Presentations on Sage-HPC (AI for HPC) and Daimos (HPC for AI)
* **Scientific Board Meeting**: Strategic discussions with board members, project leaders, and WP leaders

== Event Details

*Location*: Arts et Métiers ParisTech, 2 Cour des Arts et Métiers, 13617 Aix-en-Provence, France

*Duration*: Monday, January 19 – Wednesday, January 21, 2026

*Format*: In-person with remote access available

== Day 1: Monday, January 19

=== Welcome & WP Progress Reviews

The first day opened with a welcome lunch followed by:

* **NumPEx & ExaMA Refresher** (mandatory for newcomers)
* **Introductory Words**: Hélène Barucq and Christophe Prud'homme
* **WP1 - Discretization**: Pierre Alliez and Isabelle Ramière
* **WP2 - Model Order Reduction & Scientific ML**: Stéphane Lanteri and Emmanuel Franck
* **WP3 - Solvers for Linear Algebra**: Vincent Faucher, Luc Giraud, and Frédéric Nataf
* **WP4 - Data-Model Integration**: Mark Asch and Arthur Vidard
* **WP5 - Optimization**: El Ghazali Talbi
* **WP6 - Uncertainty Quantification**: Josselin Garnier and Clément Gauchy

=== Intra-WP Meetings

The afternoon concluded with parallel intra-WP meetings (16:30-18:00) followed by a consortium dinner.

== Day 2: Tuesday, January 20

=== Framework Presentations

Morning sessions featured detailed presentations of the project's core computational frameworks:

* **Feel++ Framework**: Christophe Prud'homme and Javier Cladellas
* **Scimba Framework**: Emmanuel Franck and Victor Michel-Dansac
* **Composyx Framework**: Luc Giraud and Arthur Gouinguenet
* **Uranie Framework**: Clément Gauchy

=== Breakout Sessions

Three rounds of targeted breakout sessions connected frameworks with relevant work packages:

**Round 1 (10:30-12:00)**:

* Feel++ × WP1 × WP2
* Composyx × WP3
* Scimba × WP5

**Round 2 (13:45-15:15)**:

* Scimba × WP1 × WP2
* Feel++ × WP4 × WP6
* Uranie × WP5

**Round 3 (15:45-17:15)**:

* Composyx × WP1 × WP2 × WP4
* Feel++ × WP3
* Uranie × WP2 × WP6

=== Scientific Board Meeting

The day concluded with a scientific board meeting (17:30-18:30) bringing together board members, project leaders, and WP leaders for strategic discussions.

== Day 3: Wednesday, January 21

=== AI-HPC Integration

Morning sessions explored the intersection of AI and HPC:

* **Sage-HPC**: AI for HPC presented by Laetitia Giraldi
* **Daimos**: HPC for AI presented by Julien Herrmann

=== Feedback & Work Planning

Each WP leader presented feedback from breakout sessions and outlined work plans:

* **WP1 Work Plan**: Pierre Alliez and Isabelle Ramière
* **WP2 Work Plan**: Stéphane Lanteri and Emmanuel Franck
* **WP3 Work Plan**: Vincent Faucher, Luc Giraud, and Frédéric Nataf
* **WP4 Work Plan**: Mark Asch and Arthur Vidard
* **WP5 Work Plan**: El Ghazali Talbi
* **WP6 Work Plan**: Josselin Garnier and Clément Gauchy

=== Closing Remarks

The assembly concluded with general closing remarks from Hélène Barucq and Christophe Prud'homme, followed by a packed lunch.

== Key Themes

=== Framework-WP Integration

The breakout sessions successfully connected computational frameworks with relevant work packages, identifying concrete collaboration opportunities and integration points.

=== AI-HPC Convergence

The Sage-HPC and Daimos presentations highlighted the bidirectional synergy between artificial intelligence and high-performance computing, a key focus for Exa-MA's evolution.

=== Cross-WP Collaboration

Multiple sessions featured collaboration across work packages (e.g., WP1 × WP2 × WP4), fostering interdisciplinary approaches to complex scientific challenges.
